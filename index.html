FAQ

Screen Enclosure FAQs

Common questions about screen enclosures, rescreening, and repairs in Gainesville, FL.

How long does a screen enclosure installation take?
Most standard patio screen enclosures are completed in one to two days. Larger pool cages or custom aluminum structures may take two to four days depending on complexity and size. We'll give you a clear timeline during your free estimate.

What type of screen material do you use?
We offer standard fiberglass screen, pet-resistant screen (3x stronger for homes with dogs or cats), solar screen (for UV and heat reduction), and no-see-um screen for smaller insects. We'll recommend the best option for your needs and budget.

Do you offer a warranty on your work?
Yes. All our installation and rescreening work is backed by a workmanship warranty. Screen materials also carry manufacturer warranties. If there's any issue with our work, we'll come back and make it right — that's our commitment to you.

How much does it cost to rescreen a patio?
Rescreening costs vary based on the size of your enclosure, the number of panels, and the screen material chosen. Most standard patio rescreening jobs run between $300 and $800. Pool cages are typically higher due to larger square footage. Contact us for an accurate free estimate — no obligation.

Are you licensed and insured to work in Florida?
Yes, absolutely. Evershade Screening & Aluminum holds a valid Florida contractor license and carries full general liability insurance and workers' compensation coverage. We're happy to provide documentation upon request before any job begins.
